# Capital Allocation Framework

Version: 1.0

## Purpose
Defines how Project Atlas allocates available capital across competing financial objectives while respecting constraints, liquidity requirements, and long-term strategy.

## Objectives
- Maximize long-term financial outcomes.
- Allocate capital consistently and transparently.
- Balance growth, protection, and liquidity.
- Produce explainable allocation decisions.

## Capital Sources
- Salary
- Bonus
- Investment income
- Rental income
- Business income
- Windfalls
- Asset sales
- Financing (where permitted)

## Allocation Priorities
1. Taxes and mandatory obligations
2. Essential living expenses
3. Emergency Fund
4. Insurance protection
5. High-interest debt repayment
6. Goal funding
7. Retirement investing
8. Long-term investment
9. Lifestyle spending

## Allocation Buckets
- Operating Cash
- Liquidity Reserve
- Debt Reduction
- Goal Funding
- Investment Portfolio
- Opportunity Capital

## Decision Inputs
- Monthly cash flow
- Available capital
- Financial Health Score
- Goal Prioritization
- Risk Capacity
- Life Stage
- Liquidity Score
- IPS constraints

## Allocation Constraints
- Preserve minimum liquidity.
- Preserve emergency reserve.
- Respect IPS allocation ranges.
- Mandatory goals override discretionary goals.
- Hard constraints cannot be violated.

## Allocation Process
1. Determine available capital.
2. Reserve mandatory allocations.
3. Evaluate competing goals.
4. Score candidate allocations.
5. Optimize remaining capital.
6. Validate constraints.
7. Generate explainable recommendation.

## Outputs
- Allocation by bucket
- Goal funding amounts
- Investment allocation
- Debt repayment recommendation
- Unallocated surplus
- Rule evaluation summary

## Business Rules
- Capital is allocated only once.
- Reserved capital cannot be double-counted.
- Windfall income follows the same framework unless explicitly overridden.
- Large allocation changes trigger scenario analysis.

## Explainability
Each recommendation includes:
- Available capital
- Allocation order
- Triggered rules
- Constraints applied
- Expected financial impact

## KPIs

- Goal Funding Ratio

- Capital Utilization Rate

# Investment Policy Statement (IPS)

## Purpose

This document defines the long-term investment philosophy, strategic rules, and portfolio governance used by Project Atlas. All investment recommendations must comply with this IPS before any optimization or scenario analysis is performed.

---

# 1. Objectives

Primary objectives:

- Preserve long-term purchasing power
- Build sustainable cash flow
- Support major life decisions
- Reduce probability of permanent capital loss
- Improve financial flexibility

---

# 2. Investment Philosophy

Core principles:

1. Invest for decades, not months.
2. Cash flow is more important than speculation.
3. Asset allocation drives long-term outcomes.
4. Risk management precedes return optimization.
5. Decisions must be systematic and repeatable.
6. Diversification is mandatory.
7. Market timing is not a primary strategy.

---

# 3. Investor Profile

Atlas supports policy-based investing for users with:

- Long investment horizons
- Regular income
- Periodic investing
- Goal-oriented portfolios
- Multiple financial objectives

---

# 4. Strategic Asset Allocation

Core asset classes:

- Cash
- Fixed Income
- Equity ETFs
- Individual Equities
- Real Estate
- Alternative Assets

Each asset class shall define:

- Target allocation
- Minimum allocation
- Maximum allocation
- Expected return
- Expected volatility
- Liquidity

---

# 5. Cash Reserve Policy

Emergency reserve should:

- Cover essential expenses
- Be highly liquid
- Remain outside investment capital
- Be replenished before increasing investment risk

---

# 6. Portfolio Construction

Portfolio design principles:

- Diversification across asset classes
- Geographic diversification
- Sector diversification
- Position sizing limits
- Concentration controls

---

# 7. Dividend Policy

Dividend income may be used to:

- Reinvest
- Support living expenses
- Increase cash reserves
- Fund major life goals

Selection should not rely solely on dividend yield.

---

# 8. Rebalancing Policy

Portfolio rebalancing may be triggered by:

- Allocation drift
- Scheduled review
- Major life event
- Risk profile change

---

# 9. Risk Policy

Primary risks:

- Market risk
- Interest-rate risk
- Liquidity risk
- Concentration risk
- Inflation risk
- Currency risk

Risk should be evaluated before expected return.

---

# 10. Prohibited Behaviors

The IPS discourages:

- Emotional investing
- Excessive leverage
- Concentrated speculative positions
- Chasing short-term performance
- Frequent unnecessary trading

---

# 11. Review Policy

The IPS should be reviewed when:

- Major family events occur
- Income materially changes
- Retirement assumptions change
- Housing strategy changes
- Long-term goals change

---

# 12. Relationship to Atlas Engines

The IPS provides governing rules for:

- Investment Engine
- Cash Flow Engine
- Retirement Engine
- Home Upgrade Engine
- Decision Engine

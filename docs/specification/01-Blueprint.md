# Purpose

The Blueprint defines the permanent financial operating model used by Project Atlas.
It describes how every major life decision is evaluated before any calculation engine is executed.

---

# 1. Decision Hierarchy

1. Life Goals
2. Financial Principles
3. Cash Flow
4. Risk Capacity
5. Asset Allocation
6. Financing Strategy
7. Individual Decisions

Lower layers must never violate higher layers.

---

# 2. Life Objectives

Primary objectives:

- Financial security
- Stable household cash flow
- Sustainable retirement
- Housing flexibility
- Family resilience
- Long-term wealth preservation

---

# 3. Financial Blueprint

Financial decisions are evaluated in the following order:

1. Emergency reserve
2. Monthly cash-flow stability
3. Insurance protection
4. Debt sustainability
5. Long-term investing
6. Lifestyle optimization

---

# 4. Cash Flow Blueprint

Priority of cash allocation:

1. Essential living expenses
2. Loan repayments
3. Emergency reserve
4. Insurance
5. Long-term investment
6. Lifestyle spending

---

# 5. Asset Blueprint

Assets are grouped into:

- Cash
- Deposits
- Stocks
- ETFs
- Bonds
- Property
- Retirement assets
- Alternative assets

Every asset shall expose:

- Value
- Liquidity
- Expected return
- Risk
- Income generation
- Tax characteristics

---

# 6. Liability Blueprint

Supported liabilities:

- Mortgage
- Personal loan
- Margin loan
- Credit card
- Other debt

Each liability includes:

- Interest rate
- Remaining term
- Monthly payment
- Outstanding balance
- Early repayment policy

---

# 7. Goal Blueprint

Supported goals include:

- Home purchase
- Home upgrade
- Retirement
- Education
- Emergency fund
- Investment target
- Debt elimination

Goals include:

- Priority
- Deadline
- Funding target
- Progress
- Dependencies

---

# 8. Risk Blueprint

Risk dimensions:

- Cash-flow risk
- Employment risk
- Interest-rate risk
- Market risk
- Concentration risk
- Liquidity risk
- Longevity risk

---

# 9. Decision Blueprint

Every recommendation follows:

Input
→ Validation
→ Financial snapshot
→ Scenario generation
→ Formula execution
→ Rule evaluation
→ Decision scoring
→ Recommendation
→ Explanation

---

# 10. Scenario Blueprint

Each scenario records:

- Assumptions
- Inflation
- Investment return
- Interest rates
- Income
- Expenses
- Tax
- Time horizon

---

# 11. Time Blueprint

Planning horizons:

- Current
- 1 year
- 3 years
- 5 years
- 10 years
- 20 years
- Retirement
- Lifetime

---

# 12. Business Rules

- Decisions must be reproducible.
- Historical assumptions are immutable.
- Recommendations require explainable evidence.
- Calculations are deterministic.
- Every scenario is versioned.

---

# 13. Outputs

Blueprint information feeds:

- Domain Model
- Formula Library
- Cash Flow Engine
- Investment Engine
- Loan Engine
- Retirement Engine
- Decision Engine

## Local-First PWA Architecture Amendment

Atlas v1 is a static PWA hosted on GitHub Pages. Domain capabilities remain unchanged. Application services execute in-process; repositories persist to IndexedDB; encrypted backup provides portability. Future Cloud Architecture adapter and Future Cloud Mapping are future optional adapters rather than v1 runtime dependencies.
